AI Assistant Workflow for Support Teams

Designed an internal AI workflow that helped support teams triage tickets faster with guardrails.

1 min read

## Context

Support agents manually classified and routed high ticket volume, causing delays and quality variance.

## Problem

Need a trustworthy AI-assisted flow that speeds triage without over-automation risk.

## Strategic artifacts

- Decision tree for risk categories and escalation boundaries.

- Success metrics balancing speed, quality, and confidence.

- Governance model for prompt updates and audit logs.

## Design artifacts

- Confidence-based UI states (auto-route, review-suggested, human-required).

- Prompt feedback panel for agent corrections.

- Reusable assistant patterns added to internal design system.

## Proof artifacts

- Time-to-triage reduced materially after pilot rollout.

- Stakeholders adopted the governance model for broader AI features.

- Live deployment in support console with weekly quality monitoring.

## Trade-offs

Chose transparent "suggested action" UX instead of hidden full automation. Slower at first, but improved trust and long-term adoption.

## Learnings

AI adoption in enterprise support succeeds when design clarifies accountability and fallback behavior.
